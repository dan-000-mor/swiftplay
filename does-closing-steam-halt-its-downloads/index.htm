Does Closing Steam Halt Its Downloads?

  • Post author:
  • Post published:December 27, 2025
  • Post category:Blog

Many users wonder whether closing the Steam client stops ongoing downloads or if they continue in the background. Understanding how Steam handles downloads when you close or minimize the application can help optimize your gaming or download experience, especially if you want to manage bandwidth or save power. This article explores the nuances of Steam’s download behavior, including how to keep games downloading without keeping Steam open and how system settings influence this process.

Steam generally pauses or suspends downloads when you are actively playing a game, especially if the game is running in the foreground. However, if you simply minimize Steam or close it using the “Exit” option, the download process often continues in the background. To ensure downloads proceed uninterrupted, you can configure specific settings within the Steam client’s Download options. For example, enabling the “Allow downloads during gameplay” feature ensures that your downloads are not paused when you’re immersed in gaming sessions. Will games download on Steam while PC is off?

The straightforward answer is no—your computer must be powered on for downloads to occur. If your PC is completely shut down or has no power, Steam cannot continue downloading files. This is because the download process requires active hardware and network connectivity. If you want to maximize download efficiency during off-hours, scheduling your PC to turn on automatically at a specified time can be beneficial. How do I keep Steam downloading while laptop lid is closed?

Laptops often suspend or hibernate when the lid is closed, which halts ongoing downloads. To continue downloading in such scenarios, adjust your power settings. On Windows, go to Power Options, then select “Choose what closing the lid does,” and set both “On battery” and “Plugged in” options to “Do nothing.” Don’t forget to click “Save Changes.” With these adjustments, your downloads can continue even when the lid is shut. Will my game download if I turn off my PC?

No, turning off your PC or putting it into standby mode will stop all ongoing downloads. While you can schedule your PC to shut down at certain times, any downloads in progress will not resume unless the system remains powered on. To keep downloads active overnight, consider configuring your PC to stay awake or sleep in a way that allows background processes to continue. Does PC keep downloading in sleep mode?

Typically, no. When a Windows PC enters sleep mode, most processes, including downloads, are paused to conserve power. The disk and network activity are usually disabled during sleep, preventing downloads from progressing. However, you can enable “Wake Timers” or set your system to stay active for updates and downloads. For detailed instructions, see this article on howtogeek.com. To avoid interruptions, some users opt to set their PCs to “Hibernate” or “Never sleep” during large downloads.

How to Continue your Downloading When your Windows 11 PC or Laptop is in Sleep Mode

To keep downloads running during sleep mode, you might need to tweak advanced power settings or use Wake-on-LAN features. But it’s often simpler to just change the sleep settings to prevent the system from sleeping during lengthy downloads. For instance, setting your PC to “Never sleep” in Power & Sleep options ensures uninterrupted downloads. Additionally, certain hardware configurations support enabling wake timers that allow the system to resume activity for updates. Do downloads continue when PC is locked?

Yes. Locking your Windows PC does not stop ongoing downloads. The system continues to run background processes, including network activity, so active downloads will persist. However, if your system enters hibernate or sleep mode, the downloads will pause until the system is active again. This feature allows you to secure your workstation without interrupting downloads.
