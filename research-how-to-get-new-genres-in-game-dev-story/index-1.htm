Research: How to Get New Genres in Game Dev Story

  • Post author:Swiftplay
  • Post published:December 26, 2025
  • Post category:Blog

Game development is a dynamic and ever-evolving industry, where innovation and diversification are key to staying competitive and engaging players worldwide. For developers seeking to expand their horizons, exploring new genres can unlock new markets, attract diverse audiences, and reinvigorate their creative processes. One of the popular simulation games that offers insight into managing a game studio and exploring genre expansion is Game Dev Story. Although it’s a simulation focused on running a game company, players often wonder how to unlock or create new genres within the game or even how to incorporate genre diversification strategies into real-world game development. This article delves into the mechanics of how to get new genres in Game Dev Story and offers strategic insights for real-world developers aiming to diversify their game offerings.

Understanding Genres in Game Dev Story

Game Dev Story simplifies the complex process of game creation by categorizing games into predefined genres. These genres influence the game’s sales, reviews, and overall success. The game features a limited set of genres, but with strategic planning, players can unlock new ones through specific actions, research, and event triggers. Understanding how genres are unlocked in the game is essential for players aiming to diversify their portfolio and improve their studio’s reputation.

How to Unlock New Genres in Game Dev Story

The core method to access new genres involves a combination of research, event triggers, and strategic decisions. Here’s a step-by-step guide to unlocking new genres in the game:

1. Conducting Research

Research is the primary method to unlock advanced genres such as Shooter, Strategy, and Simulation. To unlock these, players need to allocate resources to R&D (Research & Development) by investing in research facilities and selecting appropriate research topics. As your studio gains experience, new research options become available, unlocking more genres.

2. Managing Staff and Equipment

Having a well-rounded team with diverse skills can influence your ability to develop different genres. For example, hiring specialists in design, programming, and sound can expedite research and game development in new genres. Upgrading equipment also boosts research efficiency.

3. Triggering Special Events

In Game Dev Story, certain in-game events can unlock new genres or features. These events are often tied to milestones such as hitting specific sales targets, completing particular genres, or achieving high reputation levels. For example, reaching a certain studio reputation can trigger events that unlock new genre options.

4. Developing Genre-Specific Titles

Developing successful titles in existing genres can lead to unlocking new genres. For instance, producing a highly-rated RPG can open doors to more complex genres like Strategy or Simulation, as the game’s reputation and experience grow.

Strategies for Diversifying Your Game Portfolio

Beyond unlocking genres, effective diversification involves planning and strategic execution. Here are some proven strategies:

1. Start Small and Expand

  • Initially focus on mastering a few core genres to build your studio’s reputation.
  • Gradually invest in research and development to unlock new genres as your studio matures.
  • Balance between developing successful titles and exploring new genres.

2. Use Genre Synergies

Some genres naturally complement each other, allowing for easier diversification. For example:

Genre Pair Synergy Explanation
Adventure + Puzzle Story-driven puzzles improve engagement and replayability.
RPG + Strategy Deep narrative combined with tactical gameplay attracts dedicated players.
Music + Rhythm Seamless integration for immersive musical experiences.
Simulation + Strategy City-building or management sims with strategic elements appeal to broad audiences.

3. Analyze Market Trends

In real-world game development, understanding market trends is crucial. This involves analyzing player preferences, sales data, and emerging genres. Resources such as Newzoo provide industry analytics, helping developers decide which genres to pursue next.

4. Invest in Marketing and Reputation

A higher reputation allows you to undertake more ambitious projects, including new genres. Focus on marketing successful titles and managing user reviews to enhance your studio’s reputation. This, in turn, unlocks opportunities for exploring new genres and scaling your offerings.

Real-World Application: Scaling Your Game Development Studio

For professional developers and studios, scaling their operations to include multiple genres requires strategic planning and resource management. Building dedicated teams allows for focused expertise, faster development cycles, and higher quality output across diverse genres.

Conclusion

Unlocking and developing new genres in Game Dev Story hinges on strategic research, event management, and understanding the interplay between game mechanics and market trends. By carefully planning your studio’s growth, investing in research, and leveraging genre synergies, you can diversify your game portfolio and increase your chances of success. For real-world developers, adopting similar principles—such as building dedicated teams and scaling operations—can significantly enhance their ability to innovate and thrive in the competitive gaming landscape.
