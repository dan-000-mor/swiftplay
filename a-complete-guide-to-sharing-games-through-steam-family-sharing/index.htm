A Complete Guide to Sharing Games Through Steam Family Sharing

  • Post author:
  • Post published:December 26, 2025
  • Post category:Blog

Sharing your gaming library on Steam can be a game-changer, especially for those who want to enjoy titles without purchasing multiple copies. With the Steam Family Sharing feature, you can allow friends and family members to access your game collection, play their own achievements, and save progress to the cloud—all while maintaining control over what they can access. This guide will walk you through the entire process of setting up, inviting members, managing parental controls, and understanding the limitations of this feature, ensuring you get the most out of Steam’s sharing capabilities.

Steam’s game sharing system is particularly beneficial in today’s digital age, where the cost of games can be prohibitive for many players. While traditional borrowing of physical game discs is no longer feasible for most, digital sharing offers a flexible alternative. This feature was recently enhanced with a significant update, making it even easier to share your library securely and conveniently. Whether you’re sharing with close family members or trusted friends, understanding the setup process and restrictions is essential for a smooth experience.

Setting Up Steam Family Sharing

Steam Families allows users to share their game libraries with friends and relatives while still earning achievements and saving progress online. To utilize this feature, the shared account must be added as a family member within your Steam settings, and both parties need to be friends on Steam. Creating and managing a Steam Family is straightforward, with clear steps to follow.

First, launch the Steam application and log into your account. Once logged in, click the Steam menu at the top left corner and select Settings. In the Settings menu, navigate to the Family section on the left sidebar. Here, you’ll find options to create or join a Steam Family.

To initiate sharing, click on Create a Family. You will be prompted to name your family group—choose a name that helps you identify the shared library. After entering a name, click Create. Now, your family group is set up, and you can start inviting members.

Inviting Friends and Family to Your Steam Family

Inviting someone to your Steam Family is a simple process. Within the same Family section in Settings, select Invite a Member. Assuming the person you want to invite is already on your friends list, simply select their account from the list.

When inviting a new member, you have the option to specify whether they are an adult or a child. Inviting as a child allows you to set parental controls over their game access. After selecting the appropriate option, click Invite, and the invitation will be sent immediately.

Your friend or family member will receive a Steam notification about the invitation. They can accept it directly through Steam by clicking Accept on the invitation alert or by navigating to Steam > Family > Accept. Additionally, a confirmation email will be sent to verify their acceptance. They need to click Join Family in the email or within Steam to complete the process.

Managing Parental Controls and Playtime Limits

A standout feature of the latest Steam Families update is the enhanced parental controls. As a parent or guardian, you can now easily monitor and restrict your children’s gaming activities. To configure these controls, go to Settings > Family and Manage your Steam Family.

Select the child’s account and toggle Enable parental controls. You can then specify which games they are permitted to play by choosing Only games I select or allowing access to All games. Additionally, you can control access to community content, set playtime limits, and restrict game suggestions.

To limit screen time, enable the Set playtime limits option, which allows you to specify daily or weekly durations. These controls help ensure a healthy balance between gaming and other responsibilities, making Steam a safer environment for young gamers.

Approving Game Purchases for Children

Steam Family Sharing also simplifies the process of purchasing games for children. When a child requests a game, the adult in the family can receive a purchase request. To do this, add the desired game to your cart and click Request purchase. This sends a notification to the adult account, who can then review and approve the purchase.

To approve, the adult user should navigate to the Requests section in Steam Families, accept the purchase request, and complete the payment process. Once approved, the game will be added to the child’s library, allowing them to play immediately. This system ensures that parents retain control over spending and access.

Leaving a Steam Family or Removing Members

If you decide to leave a Steam Family group or need to remove a member, the process is equally straightforward. To exit a family, go to Settings > Family, then click Manage your Family. From there, select Leave Family.

To remove a specific member, access the same menu, find the member’s name, and click Remove Member. Keep in mind that once you leave a family group, you must wait at least a year before rejoining the same group, so choose carefully.

Limitations of Steam Family Sharing

While Steam Family Sharing offers a practical way to share your game library, it does have certain limitations. You can link up to six accounts within your family group, but sharing is only possible with accounts in the same region—meaning accounts must reside in the same country. Additionally, you can choose to share your entire library or none at all; partial sharing isn’t supported.

Not all games support sharing, particularly those with specific DRM restrictions or online multiplayer requirements. Moreover, only one person can access and play a shared game at a time. If multiple users try to play the same game, priority is given to the account that is currently sharing it. If someone else is playing, the other user will be prompted to save their progress and quit.
