Food Benefits and Nutrition Support

Food assistance programs administered by Oregon DHS aim to combat hunger and promote nutritional health among vulnerable populations. The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP), formerly known as food stamps, provides eligible households with funds to purchase nutritious food. Applicants must meet income and resource guidelines, and the application process involves completing forms and providing proof of income, expenses, and household size. Benefits are typically distributed via Electronic Benefit Transfer (EBT) cards, which can be used at approved grocery stores and farmers’ markets.
